Case Study:Plutopay

Overview

Plutopay provides a platform for people to settle digital payments and set their budget plan. The specific features being tested in this study include sending & requesting money, creating a community, and budgeting money.

The problem

People are annoyed by using different kinds of cards for each transaction. They are having concerns over privacy and confidentiality. People face difficulties in managing a pool money. The existing app lack of saving and goal setting functions

The Solution

Allow multiple cards store in the app Setting two-factor authentication for each transaction Adding a group function that allows users to from groups for transactions Allowing users to create their own saving plan and monitor their saving progress

Problem Statement

Our users need a secure and stable way to transfer, save, and move money by storing multiple cards in the application at the same time because they want to manage their money and financial habits. We will know this to be true when we see the number of successfully transactions of this application

Competitive Analysis

I have conduct competitive research on two existing app including Alipay and PayMe, and analysis to identify what solutions exist and where our app might be able to improve.

Persona

Based on the research I set up two personas. We referred to them throughout the entire product development process.

User Stories

User Story One - Form a community to send and receive payment request

As an outgoing person, Kate wants to find a way to split the pool money in the friend gathering, so that she can save time in calculating and collecting the pool money.

Entry Point: Recommended by friend

  1. Sign Up
  2. Find how to create a group
  3. Add group members
  4. Browse payment option
  5. Send bill request to her friends

Success Criteria: Receive payment from group members

User Story Two - Payment

As a busy banker, Celia wants to find an app that can handle all of the miscellaneous expenses from different saving category, so that she can save time for her kids and have more rest time.

Saw the advertisement

  1. Open Plutopay app
  2. Store multiple cards to the e-wallet
  3. Create saving plan
  4. Choose the corresponding saving category to pay
  5. Fill in the payment information

Success Criteria: Pay all the miscellaneous expenses

User Story Three - Save money to a budget plan

As a shrewd woman, Celia wants to find a place for categorizing her saving, so that she can spend money in an organized way.

Saw the advertisement

  1. Make an account on Plutopay
  2. Browse recommended saving plan
  3. Create a new category
  4. Enter the amount that she wants to save
  5. Transfer money from the remaining balance or cards

Success Criteria: Save money to the saving category

Card Sorting

Purpose

I decided on the main categories of the e-wallet app with a Card Sorting session. I have invited 5 people to do the test through Optimal Workshop. All of them has completed the whole test and do it for around 2-4 minutes. I adopted the closed card sort method

Result

After finishing the card sorting test, I have made a few amendments on Plutopay Sitemap.

Usability Test

This is a moderated remote testing. 5 participants were recruited through personal network to participate in usability testing, some of them having experience in using digital wallets and some of them no.

Business Requirements and Goals

Goals

The goal of this study is to observe the usability and utility of Plutopay app features. We will also assess users’ satisfaction and learnability-wallet with people who haven’t tried to use e-wallet before.

Test Objectives

  • The specific features being tested in this study include sending & requesting money, creating a community, and budgeting money. During the usability test, these answers are seeked to find out.
  • Is our app intuitive and learnable for all users?
  • How easily can users receive money from and transfer to other users?
  • How useful and easy is the community function to users?
  • Can users easily create and edit in the budgeting feature?s
  • What could be improved in the app?

Final Takeaways

Roadblocks

Overall, I really enjoy working in this project because it provides me many learning opportunities on the fintech industry. The roadblock that I encounter is the technology part. It is difficult to prove my design feasibility without any technology professions to work along with me. So I prevent from making some brand new function is avoid this problem. Also, the UI design of this project is not fully detailed due to time constraint. I would definitely create a more perfect app if I have more time.

What I Learned

I learned that building a style guide will definitely help me to create a more organized design. The feasibility of design flow is important because we have to pass the design to the developer and let them to implement it. Also, before finalising the user flow, it is better to design all the stuff in balck and white, which is an efficient way to create the detailed UI.
